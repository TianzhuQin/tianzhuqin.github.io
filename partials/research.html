Research Agenda

Understanding climate, cognition, and collective response through data-rich inquiry

01

Environmental Policy Effect Estimation

Quantifying how regulatory interventions influence climate adaptation, emissions, and public goods delivery using quasi-experimental and panel causal inference designs.

  • Spatiotemporal causal estimators
  • Heterogeneous policy impact profiling
  • Evidence synthesis for climate accountability
02

Human-centered AI System Deployment

Designing sociotechnical guardrails that keep AI aligned with stakeholder needs, emphasizing transparency, interpretability, and collaborative oversight in the loop.

  • Participatory evaluation frameworks
  • Operational interpretability tooling
  • Deployment playbooks for public agencies
03

Societal Response to Climate Action

Mapping how communities interpret and disseminate information about climate crises, including the role AI agents and media platforms play in shaping collective narratives.

  • Agent-based modeling of information diffusion
  • Computational ethnography of digital publics
  • Climate mis/disinformation mitigation
